Day 2: Storage Devices and Memory

Goals:

Understand different types of storage devices.

Learn the differences between RAM and permanent storage.

Motives:

To comprehend how data is stored and accessed in a computer.

Detailed Information:

RAM vs. ROM: Differences and Usage

RAM (Random Access Memory):

  • Volatile Memory: Loses data when the power is off.
  • Speed: Very fast read and write speeds.
  • Usage: Temporary data storage for running applications and processes.

ROM (Read-Only Memory):

  • Non-Volatile Memory: Retains data even when the power is off.
  • Speed: Slower compared to RAM.
  • Usage: Permanent storage for firmware and BIOS.

HDD vs. SSD: Speed, Durability, and Capacity Differences

HDD (Hard Disk Drive):

  • Speed: Slower read/write speeds due to mechanical parts.
  • Durability: More prone to physical damage due to moving parts.
  • Capacity: Generally larger capacities at lower costs.

SSD (Solid State Drive):

  • Speed: Much faster read/write speeds due to lack of moving parts.
  • Durability: More durable as there are no moving parts.
  • Capacity: Generally more expensive per GB but getting more affordable.

External Storage Devices:

USB Drives:

  • Usage: Portable and convenient for transferring data.
  • Capacity: Ranges from a few GBs to several hundred GBs.

External Hard Drives:

  • Usage: For backup and extra storage.
  • Capacity: Larger capacities, typically from 500GB to several TB.

Cloud Storage:

  • Usage: Access data from anywhere with an internet connection.
  • Capacity: Varies by provider, often scalable.

Activity: Speed Comparison Experiment

Objective: Compare the boot times of computers with HDDs and SSDs.

Procedure:

  • Set up two computers, one with an HDD and one with an SSD.
  • Measure and record the time taken for each computer to boot up from a cold start.
  • Compare and analyze the results.

Quiz:

  1. What is the difference between RAM and ROM?
  2. Which storage device is faster: HDD or SSD?
  3. Name two types of external storage devices.
