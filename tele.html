Network and Telecommunication

Introduction to Networking

Networking is the practice of connecting computers and other devices to share resources. It can be done using cables (wired networking) or wirelessly.

Types of Networks

There are several types of networks, including LAN (Local Area Network), WAN (Wide Area Network), and MAN (Metropolitan Area Network).

Network Topologies

Network topology refers to the arrangement of different elements (links, nodes, etc.) in a computer network. Common topologies include star, ring, bus, and mesh.

Wireless Networking

Wireless networking allows devices to connect to the network without physical cables. It uses technologies like Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and cellular networks.

Internet and Intranet

The internet is a global network connecting millions of private, public, academic, business, and government networks. An intranet is a private network accessible only to an organization's staff.

Future of Networking

Emerging technologies like 5G, IoT (Internet of Things), and AI (Artificial Intelligence) are shaping the future of networking and telecommunication.
